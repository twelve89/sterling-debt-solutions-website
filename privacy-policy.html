Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2026

Sterling Debt Solutions ("Sterling," "we," "us," or "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information we collect in connection with our debt collection and receivables management services. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, share, and safeguard information about consumers who interact with us, including visitors to our website and users of our consumer account portal.

1. Scope of This Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to personal information we collect through our website, our consumer account portal, phone calls, written correspondence, and other interactions with consumers and clients. It does not apply to information collected by third parties whose websites may be linked from our site.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information from several sources in order to perform our debt collection services and comply with applicable laws:

Information we receive from our clients

When a creditor places an account with us for collection, we typically receive information including your name, address, telephone number, Social Security number (in some cases), account balance, account history, and other information necessary to service the account.

Information you provide to us

  • Contact details such as name, mailing address, email address, and phone number
  • Payment information, including bank account or card details you provide when making a payment
  • Information you provide when communicating with us about your account or a dispute
  • Information submitted through our contact form or consumer portal

Information we collect automatically

When you visit our website, we automatically collect certain technical information, including IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URL, pages viewed, and timestamps. We may use cookies and similar technologies to operate the website and analyze traffic.

Information from third parties

Consistent with applicable law and for permissible purposes, we may obtain information from credit bureaus, skip tracing providers, public records, and other lawful sources in order to locate consumers and service accounts.

3. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect for purposes that include:

  • Collecting debts and servicing accounts placed with us
  • Verifying consumer identity and responding to disputes
  • Processing payments and payment arrangements
  • Communicating with consumers about their accounts
  • Complying with legal, regulatory, and reporting obligations including the FDCPA and FCRA
  • Maintaining the security and integrity of our systems
  • Improving our services and consumer experience

4. How We Share Information

We do not sell personal information. We may share information with:

  • Our clients (original creditors or debt owners) for the purpose of reporting on account activity
  • Service providers who assist us with payment processing, mailing, technology hosting, skip tracing, legal services, and similar functions
  • Credit reporting agencies, consistent with the Fair Credit Reporting Act
  • Regulators, courts, and government agencies as required by law or legal process
  • Other parties when we have your consent, or in connection with a business transfer

5. Data Security

We maintain administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. Sensitive information transmitted through our consumer portal is encrypted using industry-standard transport layer security. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, however, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, including to comply with legal, regulatory, tax, accounting, and reporting obligations, to resolve disputes, and to enforce our agreements.

7. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information, including the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, to request correction of inaccurate information, and to request that we delete certain information, subject to legal and contractual limitations.

Note: Some rights may be limited because we are required by law to maintain certain records related to debt collection activity. To exercise a privacy right, contact us using the information at the bottom of this page.

8. Children's Privacy

Our website and services are not directed to children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

9. Third-Party Websites

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of any third-party site. We encourage you to review the privacy policy of any website you visit.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Effective Date" above. Material changes will be communicated through our website. Your continued use of our services after any update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
