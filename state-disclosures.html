State Disclosures

Last Updated: January 1, 2026

Sterling Debt Solutions is committed to full compliance with federal and state debt collection laws. Certain states require debt collection agencies to provide specific disclosures to consumers. Those disclosures are set out below. Nothing in this page is intended to limit any rights you may have under federal law, including the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA).

Michigan

Sterling Debt Solutions is headquartered in Michigan and is subject to the Michigan Occupational Code (MCL 339.901 et seq.), which regulates collection practices by licensed collection agencies operating in the state, as well as the Michigan Collection Practices Act (MCL 445.251 et seq.), which prohibits certain practices by creditors and collectors regarding debts owed by Michigan residents.

Michigan Consumer Rights

Under Michigan law, Michigan residents have certain rights when dealing with debt collectors, including the right to:

  • Be treated fairly and without harassment, oppression, or abuse
  • Receive accurate information about the debt, including the name of the original creditor, the amount owed, and your right to dispute
  • Request that communication stop, with limited exceptions
  • Not be contacted at unusual or inconvenient times or places
  • File a complaint with the Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA)

General Federal Notice

In addition to state-specific laws, all communications from Sterling Debt Solutions are governed by the federal Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA), 15 U.S.C. § 1692 et seq., and the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), 15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq. For a summary of your rights under these laws, please see our Consumer Rights page.

If you believe your rights under federal law have been violated, you may file a complaint with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau at www.consumerfinance.gov or with the Federal Trade Commission at www.ftc.gov.
